## Description

Loomi is a companion-led wellness app designed to make stress relief feel
approachable and personal. Through guided breathing, grounding exercises, and
daily mood check-ins, Loomi helps users pause, reset, and build healthier
emotional habits in just a few minutes a day.

THE STRESS BUTTON (always free)
- Guided breathing (in for 4, out for 6 — slows your heart rate fast)
- 5-4-3-2-1 grounding to pull a racing mind back to right now
- A short reframe to name what's weighing on you
- A Home Screen widget and Action Button shortcut so it's one tap away

PRIVATE BY DESIGN
- No account, no sign-up, no ads
- Your journal and check-ins are stored only on your phone — never uploaded

LOOMI+ (optional)
- On-device insights into your own patterns — your toughest days, whether
  the relief flow is actually helping — computed on your phone, never sent
  anywhere
- Your full check-in history, not just this week

Crisis resources and the core relief flow are free, forever. Loomi is a
self-help companion for everyday stress, not a replacement for a doctor or
therapist.

## What to measure (this experiment's success criteria)

No analytics backend exists by design, so measure manually for 2-4 weeks
after publishing using App Store Connect's free impression/conversion charts,
review count/rating, and any organic mentions. If impressions stay near zero,
the listing isn't the bottleneck — discovery is — and the next experiment
should be seeding one niche community (per the roadmap), not more ASO tweaks.

## Notes for whoever fills in the rest

- Do not add ratings/review counts, "Editors' Choice" or similar badges, or
  "trusted by X users" claims until they're real — pre-launch, none of this
  exists yet.
- Avoid "science-backed" / "evidence-based" as a claim about the *app* — the
  underlying techniques (paced breathing, grounding) have real support, but
  the app itself hasn't been clinically validated. Keep claims at the
  technique level, not the product level.
- Never mention or imply crisis/medical claims beyond "self-help companion,
  not medical care" — see Support screen copy in the app itself.
