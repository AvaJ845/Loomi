# Distribution experiment — draft

Per the roast verdict: at zero installs and zero marketing budget, broad
ASO/App Store discovery won't get anywhere near $500/mo. The tractable path
is a small number of high-intent buyers found through a specific niche, not
the general "stress relief" category.

## Pick one niche first, not a broad audience

Best candidate from the buyer roleplay last round: **shift workers** (nurses,
ER/ICU staff, EMTs, factory/warehouse night shifts) — a group with
above-average occupational stress, an existing pattern of looking for
stress-relief tools, and active, identifiable online communities. Don't
spread across multiple niches at once; pick this one, run it, see what
sticks before trying a second.

## Where to post (communities, not ads)

- Reddit: r/nursing, r/ems, r/NewToEMS, r/Nightshift — *read each community's
  self-promotion rules first*; most require participating genuinely before
  posting anything that looks like marketing, and some ban app promotion
  outright. A "what do you use to decompress after a shift" discussion post
  (no link) is safer first move than a direct app post.
- Facebook groups for nurses/shift workers — same rule: check group rules,
  many explicitly disallow promotional posts from new accounts.
- A short demo video/GIF of the breathe→ground→reframe flow is more shareable
  than a text post — show, don't describe.

## Draft post (for a context where self-promotion is allowed, or as a comment in a relevant thread)

> Made a small app called Loomi for getting through stressful moments at
> work — one-tap breathing, a 5-4-3-2-1 grounding exercise, and a private
> on-device journal (nothing leaves your phone, no account, no tracking).
> Built it because most stress apps I tried either wanted a subscription
> before I'd even tried it or felt clinical. This one's free for the actual
> relief tools — no catch. Would genuinely like feedback from people who
> deal with this for a living. [link]

Notes on the draft:
- Leads with the free, no-catch core tools — credibility before any ask.
- "No account, no tracking" is the real differentiator from Rootd — say it
  plainly, don't oversell it.
- Ends with a feedback ask, not a download ask — lower-friction and more
  likely to get an actual response in a community context.
- Swap "at work" framing per community (EMS vs. nursing vs. night-shift
  retail) — keep the rest the same.

## What "running it" looks like

1. Spend a few days genuinely participating in 1-2 of the communities above
   before posting anything app-related — cold self-promotion gets removed
   and burns the account.
2. Post the draft (or a community-appropriate variant) once, in one place.
3. Track: clicks/installs if there's a way to (a simple link shortener with
   click counts is enough — no need for an attribution SDK), and direct
   replies/feedback.
4. Don't repeat in the same community — if it doesn't land, that's a result
   (try a different niche or framing), not a reason to repost.

This is a draft for you to review, adjust in your own voice, and actually
post — that part has to be a real human in a real community, not something
to automate.
